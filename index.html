How We Got Here

1870s
Wolves had lived here for thousands of years. They kept elk moving, plants growing, and the river healthy. Everything was connected.
Balancing Loop — "The ecosystem ran itself like a thermostat."
1926
By 1926, every wolf in Yellowstone had been killed by hunters and ranchers. Nobody realized what would happen next.
Reinforcing Loop begins — "Without the balancing loop, a runaway loop took over."
1930s – 1980s
Elk stopped moving. They ate the riverbanks bare. Without roots, the banks crumbled. The river spread wide and muddy. Beavers, birds, and fish disappeared.
Reinforcing Loop — "Fewer plants → more erosion → even fewer plants. A vicious circle with no brake."
1995
Scientists drove 14 wolves in from Canada. Almost immediately, elk changed their behavior — avoiding the riverbanks where wolves could ambush them. That fear changed everything.
Balancing Loop — "The 'landscape of fear' created a new balancing loop."
Today
The wolves didn't just change the elk. They changed the rivers. This is called a trophic cascade — one change at the top ripples all the way down.
Reinforcing → Balancing — "A vicious circle became a virtuous circle, then settled into balance."
